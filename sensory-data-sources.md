# Non-Obvious Data Sources for Central Park Recreation
## Compiled March 2026

### Sound
- Freesound.org: "Central Park Ambience.aif" by logancircle2 (CC-BY 4.0)
- SONYC dataset: 18,510 tagged urban audio recordings (CC-BY 4.0, Zenodo)
- David Haskell: Central Park recording from "Sounds Wild and Broken" (SoundCloud)
- Ellen Reid SOUNDWALK: 25 GPS-triggered musical zones across all 840 acres
- DOT National Transportation Noise Map: traffic noise contours around perimeter

### Olmsted's Design Intent (LOC, public domain)
- **Pastoral** (Meadows, lake edges): "soothe and restore the spirit"
- **Picturesque** (Ramble, North Woods): "sense of mystery," winding paths, layered shade
- **Formal** (Mall, Bethesda, Conservatory Garden): "open-air reception hall"
- Library of Congress: 24,000 items, 47,300 digitized images of Olmsted papers

### Phenology / Seasons
- USA-NPN Spring Indices: bloom/leaf-out timing (GIS layers)
- CP Conservancy Fall Foliage Tracker: daily updates, 18,000+ trees
- Cherry blossoms: Yoshino (white, late March), Kwanzan (pink, May)
- NYBG Flora: 438 species across 36 zones

### Night Sky (Bortle 8-9)
- Nearly starless amber dome — only planets + ~10 brightest stars visible
- Sky glow: warm amber-brown, never fully dark
- No Milky Way visible, ever

### Weather (NOAA station since 1869)
- Prevailing wind: NW in winter, S/SW in summer
- CP Climate Lab: woodlands 10°F cooler than streets on hottest days
- Microclimate cooling greater at night

### Geology (AMNH field guide, free PDF)
- Manhattan schist: mica flakes create sparkle in sunlight (shader detail)
- 10 mapped outcrop locations with geological descriptions
- Glacial grooves generally N-S direction

### Citizen Science
- eBird hotspot L191106: one of world's most-observed birding locations
- iNaturalist: all taxa with GPS + photos
- NYC 311 noise complaints: noise density around perimeter

### Historical Reference
- NYPL stereoscopic views: 3D photo pairs of historical park structures
- 1859 topography map: original terrain before Olmsted's landscaping
- Otto Sibeth species map: 642 species locations in the park
